01.

The Story So Far

I didn't start out planning to work with data. I started with machines — the nuts-and-bolts, thermodynamics-and-stress-analysis kind. IIT Roorkee's Mechanical Engineering programme was rigorous, structured, and precise. It taught me to think in systems.

Somewhere between the equations and the labs, I realised the most interesting problems weren't in the machinery itself — they were in the decisions being made around it. Why does a factory keep running equipment until it breaks? Why do companies spend more acquiring customers than keeping them? Why does a political campaign go door-to-door when data can tell you which door actually matters?

That curiosity pulled me toward data science, then toward strategy, then toward consulting. Not because I was chasing a title — but because I wanted to be in the room where the hard calls get made, and I wanted to be the one who could back those calls up with evidence.

Today I approach every problem the way a founder would: obsessing over the "so what" more than the methodology, moving fast with whatever signal is available, and always asking — does this actually change how someone decides something?

Management Trainee Intern

My first taste of what industrial data can actually do. TATA Steel's blast furnaces are enormous, expensive, and when they break unexpectedly the costs spiral fast. I was tasked with building a system that could see the failure coming before it happened.

Working with the plant's technical team, I combined IoT sensor data, vibration analysis, and ML models into a predictive maintenance pipeline for blast furnace screens. The system went from pilot to plant-wide adoption over the summer — cutting unscheduled shutdown costs by ₹40 lakh and shaving 10 hours off maintenance downtime.

  • Leveraged vibration signatures integrated with IoT sensor feeds for proactive early machine failure detection
